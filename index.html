Q: What is a General Measurement System and what are its key components?
A: A general measurement system consists of sensing, signal conditioning, signal processing, and data presentation elements. It connects an observer to a process by transforming real-world variables into interpretable information.

Q: How are sensors classified?
A: Sensors are classified by the physical quantity measured, operating principle, signal type (analog/digital), power requirement (active/passive), and whether they operate by contact or not.

Q: What are "static characteristics" of sensors and why are they important?
A: These describe sensor behavior under steady-state conditions and include range, span, sensitivity, non-linearity, resolution, hysteresis, and calibration accuracy.

Q: What are "dynamic characteristics" of sensors and when do they become important?
A: Dynamic characteristics describe how sensors respond to rapidly changing inputs. They are modeled using differential equations and include time constant, damping ratio, and frequency response.

Q: What are loading effects in measurement systems and how are input and output impedance related to them?
A: Loading effects occur when a sensor draws energy from the system it measures, affecting accuracy. High input impedance minimizes this, and impedance matching improves signal transfer.

Q: How do resistive elements function as sensors for displacement and strain?
A: These change resistance when stretched or compressed. Potentiometers and strain gauges are common examples used to measure displacement and strain.

Q: How do capacitive elements function as sensors for displacement and liquid level?
A: They detect changes in capacitance due to displacement or fluid level. This may occur by changing the plate distance, area of overlap, or dielectric material.

Q: What are Linear Variable Differential Transformers (LVDTs) and how do they measure displacement?
A: LVDTs are non-contact sensors that convert linear displacement into an electrical signal using mutual inductance and AC excitation.

Q: How do Hall Effect Sensors detect magnetic fields?
A: Hall sensors detect magnetic fields via the Hall voltage generated when a current-carrying conductor is placed in a perpendicular magnetic field.
