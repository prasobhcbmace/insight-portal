Q: What is a General Measurement System and what are its key components?
A: A system linking an observer to a process with four elements: sensing, signal conditioning, signal processing, and presentation. Often combined in a transducer.

Q: How are sensors classified?
A: Based on quantity measured, operating principle, signal type, power need, and contact type.

Q: What are "static characteristics" of sensors?
A: Range, span, transfer function, non-linearity, sensitivity, resolution, calibration, and hysteresis ‚Äî crucial for steady-state performance.

Q: What are "dynamic characteristics" and when are they important?
A: Response to rapidly changing inputs. Includes time constant (1st order), damping ratio, and resonance (2nd order).

Q: What are loading effects and impedance in sensors?
A: Loading effects occur when sensors draw energy. Input/output impedance governs signal transfer efficiency.

Q: How do resistive elements function as sensors?
A: Potentiometers for displacement; strain gauges for deformation, both rely on change in resistance.

Q: How do capacitive sensors measure displacement and level?
A: Capacitance changes due to area, separation, or dielectric variation. Liquid level sensors use coaxial cylinders.

Q: What are LVDTs and their working principle?
A: Linear inductive transducers. AC excitation in a primary coil induces voltages in secondaries that vary with core position.

Q: How do Hall Effect Sensors detect fields?
A: Charge carriers deflect in a magnetic field, generating a transverse voltage proportional to the field strength.
